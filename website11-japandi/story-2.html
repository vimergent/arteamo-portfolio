II. Material

The honest beauty of wood, stone, and linen

True luxury lies not in ornament but in authenticity. Each material we select tells its own story—the grain of oak speaking of decades of growth, the weave of linen carrying the memory of flax fields, the coolness of stone holding millennia of geological time.

White oak flooring, hand-finished with natural oils

In the Japandi philosophy, materials are left to express their natural character. Wood is oiled rather than lacquered, allowing the grain to be felt as well as seen. Fabrics are chosen for their texture and drape, not merely their appearance. Every surface invites touch.

Belgian linen and Japanese cotton create tactile landscapes

We embrace the concept of wabi-sabi—finding beauty in imperfection and impermanence. A knot in wood becomes a focal point. The irregular edge of handmade ceramics adds character. These "flaws" remind us that our spaces are alive, changing with time and use.

Local limestone paired with handcrafted ceramics

Color emerges naturally from materials rather than applied pigments. The warm honey of pine, the cool grey of concrete, the soft white of raw cotton—these form our palette. Accent colors, when used, come from nature: the sage of eucalyptus, the terracotta of clay.

A palette drawn from earth and sky
