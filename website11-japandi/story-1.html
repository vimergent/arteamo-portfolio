I. Light

How natural illumination shapes living spaces

In Japanese aesthetics, light is not merely illumination—it is a material as tangible as wood or stone. The play of shadows, the gentle gradation from brightness to darkness, these become architectural elements in their own right.

Morning light filtered through linen, Apartment K55, 2021

Our approach begins with understanding the path of the sun. Each room is oriented to capture specific qualities of light throughout the day. Eastern exposures welcome the gentle morning rays, while northern light provides consistent, soft illumination for workspaces.

The interplay of light and shadow creates ever-changing art

We employ layers of translucency—sheer curtains, rice paper screens, frosted glass—to diffuse and soften harsh sunlight. This creates an atmosphere of perpetual dawn or dusk, where time seems to slow and spaces breathe with calm.

Transitioning from natural to artificial light seamlessly

As daylight fades, carefully positioned artificial lighting takes over, maintaining the same quality of gentle illumination. Hidden LED strips, paper lanterns, and dimmable fixtures ensure that the magic of natural light continues into the evening hours.
